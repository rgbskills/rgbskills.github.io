I have a lot of years behind me (10) and I was always looking for the best and the most perfect solution with focus on the user experience and the application interface.

I overtest my work, sometimes Just to make sure that anyone can use the application I design and build. I test them with people who never used a computer. My code quality is very good, I comment my code and use a semantic approach so any developer after me or working with me, will understand it.

My workflow

I'm a very good team player, with a proffesional attitude and I get along well with people.

I start by analyzing what the client wants from the project, and since he is (in most cases) a layperson, he doesn't really know what he needs exactly, so you have to walk him through the process and sometimes tweak his thoughts and expectations and show him what he really needs and how to get the best results.

I have a good system in finding the best and the most simple way of getting things done. I don't like to double my work time, so for me it's very important to agree on something with the client before I start coding. That's how I make my clients happy.

Something that needs to be explained is not done right. This is where the user interface comes in. It's my job to make it as simple as possible.

The user experience is another very important aspect when creating a project. Some analytics are required here to sniff out the target audience and tracking moves so you can further enhance the user's experience.
